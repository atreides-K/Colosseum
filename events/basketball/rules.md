# 🏀 Basketball Rules — Spectrum '26

## Rules of the Game

### Time-Based

1. The game consists of **4 quarters of 10 minutes** each. Running time for group stage (clock stops only for free throws and fouls), except the final minute of the final quarter which runs on referee time. Finals run on referee time throughout.
2. **Overtime:** 5 minutes if scores tied after regulation. Subsequent overtimes: 2 minutes each.
3. Break between quarters: 2 minutes. Half-time: 5 minutes.
4. **Timeouts:** 1 minute each. One per quarter for first three quarters, 2 in the final quarter. Can only be called when team has possession.
5. **24-second shot clock**, resets to 14 seconds on hitting the offensive rim.
6. Ball must cross half-court within **8 seconds** of live ball.
7. **5 seconds** for throw-in once referee hands ball to player; 5 seconds for free throw.
8. Player has **8 seconds** to start a dribble when holding the ball, and **5 seconds** to release after stopping a dribble.
9. **3-second violation:** No player may stay in the opponent's restricted area for more than 3 consecutive seconds while their team controls a live ball in the front court.
10. Bench will blow whistle at end of quarter time, countdown last 10 seconds, and give heads-up at 1 minute and 30 seconds remaining.
11. If the ball has left contact with a player before time expires and the shot goes in, it counts.

### Fouls

1. All fouls based on **FIBA rules** (offensive charge, reaching, etc.).
2. **4 team fouls** = 2 free throws. Every subsequent foul on a non-shooting player grants 2 free throws. Team foul count resets each quarter. **5 individual fouls** = ejection.
3. All referee calls are final and not subject to change.
4. Teams arguing with a referee may receive a **technical foul**.
5. Bench must maintain distance from sideline as directed by referees — violation results in a technical foul.

## Team Composition

1. Minimum **5 members**, maximum **12 members**.
2. Substitutions allowed during timeouts or intervals — unlimited substitutions from the 12-member roster.
3. If a team cannot field 5 players within **15 minutes** of scheduled time, it results in a forfeit.
4. **2 guest players** allowed per roster for the entire tournament. Guest cannot play for any other team. Maximum **1 guest on court** at any time. Guests may be current institute members or alumni.

## General Conduct

1. Proper sports/basketball shoes and attire required. No full-sleeved shirts — sleeves must end above elbows. Shirts must be tucked in.
2. Toss winner chooses side or bibs; other team takes the remaining choice.
3. **Nails must be clipped.** No jewelry or accessories (watches, bangles, etc.) while playing.
4. Teams unable to make a scheduled match must inform conveners **24 hours** before. Failure to do so results in a 100-point penalty from overall department Spectrum score.

## Tournament Structure

- **9–11 teams:** 3 pools (chit-picking), round-robin within pools. Pool winners → semifinals. 4th semifinalist decided by round-robin among 2nd-place teams. Seeded by point difference: 1v3 and 2v4.
- **12+ teams:** 4 pools with at least 3 teams each, round-robin. Pool winners → semifinals, seeded by point difference: 1v3 and 2v4.
- **Fewer than 9 teams:** 2 pools, round-robin. 1st place teams → semifinals. 2nd of Pool A vs 3rd of Pool B (and vice versa) for remaining semifinal spots. Seeded by point difference: 1v3 and 2v4.

**Point difference:** +PD for winning team, −PD for losing team, accumulated across all matches.

> For any rule not listed here, the **FIBA handbook** will be referred to. Rules are subject to change upon discussion and agreement with all captains.
