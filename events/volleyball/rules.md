# 🏐 Volleyball Rules — Spectrum '26

## Eligibility

Open tournament for all **IISc community members** with valid ID: students, staff, professors, post-docs, research associates, project associates, alumni, interns, security, mess workers, Samadhan staff, and anyone with a valid IISc or Gymkhana ID card.

Family members of staff/faculty not directly related to IISc are **not eligible** (unless they hold a valid Gymkhana ID).

## Team Composition

- Up to **12 players** per team
- Each player enrolled in **one team only** — no external participants
- Maximum **2 teams per department**
- Player details submitted via Google form; verification before each game

## Guest Player Rules

- Any eligible person playing for a department other than their own is a **guest player**
- **Alumni** are guests in all departments
- Maximum **2 guest players on the ground** at any time
- Once a guest plays for a department, they **cannot play for any other** team
- Even playing for **1 point** locks the guest to that team
- Guests can be substituted with other guests without breaking the 2-on-field limit

## Tournament Structure

- Teams divided into **pools** → league matches (+ knockouts if needed) → quarterfinals (if needed) → semifinals → 3rd-place match → final
- After registration, teams may withdraw before knockout/league matches

## Match Format

- League/knockout: **Best of 3 sets** (25-25-15 points)
- Semis/finals: **Best of 3 or 5 sets** (by agreement between teams)

## Game Rules

- **FIVB rules** with the exception of **no rotation** (fixed player positions)

## Scoring (League)

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Win | 2 |
| Technical forfeit | 1 |
| Loss | 0 |

## Tiebreak

In case of tied league scores: **sets won** → **point difference** → team with the most sets won or highest point difference advances.

## Schedule

- Matches scheduled **5 PM – 12 AM**
- Holiday morning hours may also be used if needed
- Organizing committee reserves the right to change schedule

## Match Day Rules

- **10-minute** grace period before walkover
- Team refusing to play without valid reason = **eliminated** (previous results voided)
- **No on-spot registrations**

## Code of Conduct

- No abuse or quarreling with opponents or referee
- Warnings given first to player and captain; continued violations → **team disqualified** from Spectrum
- Referee's decision is **final** — serious disputes may result in disqualification

## Complaints

- Must be raised with referee or organizing committee **before or during** the match
- Post-match complaints **will not be entertained**
- Any team found cheating or violating rules → **immediate disqualification**
