# 🤾 Handball Rules — Spectrum '26

## 1. Scoring

A goal is scored when the whole of the ball passes between the goalposts and travels fully over the goal line. A goal is not awarded until the referee has signalled and is confident no rules have been broken.

At the end of the match, the team with the most goals wins. If both teams have the same score, officials will decide whether extra time or a penalty shoot-out determines the winner.

## 2. Match Duration

- **League game:** 2 x 10-minute halves, 5-minute break
- **Semi-final:** 2 x 15-minute halves, 5-minute break
- **Final:** 2 x 20-minute halves, 7-minute break

## 3. General Rules

- A team cannot keep possession without attempting to attack
- The start is awarded to the team that wins the coin toss
- A match begins with both teams in their own half
- A goal can be scored from any type of throw

### Ball Handling

- A player can run with the ball for **3 steps maximum**
- A player can hold the ball for up to **3 seconds maximum**
- A player can continuously dribble, providing they bounce the ball
- A player can take 3 steps maximum before and after dribbling (no "double dribble")

### Fouls & Contact

- Players are not able to endanger an opponent with the ball
- Players are not permitted to pull, hit or punch the ball out of an opponent's hands
- Players cannot make contact with the ball below the knee
- Players cannot dive on the floor to regain a loose ball
- A player is allowed to use the torso to obstruct an opponent with or without the ball
- A player cannot outstretch arms or legs to obstruct, push, hold, trip or hit
- An attacking player is not allowed to charge into a defensive player

### Throw-ins & Free Throws

- A throw-in is awarded when the ball goes out of bounds; the thrower must place one foot on the side-line to execute the throw
- **All opposing players must stay 3 m away from the throw-in**
- All minor fouls or violations are penalised with a free-throw taken at the place of infringement

## 4. Officials

A game is officiated by four officials — two referees, a scorekeeper, and a timekeeper. The referees are positioned diagonally on court to observe a side line each. Depending on who is attacking, one is called the field referee and the other the goal referee.

Officials use a whistle and 18 hand signals to communicate decisions, and use a yellow and red card system to warn or remove players.

## 5. Playing Area

A competitive handball court is **40 m long x 20 m wide**, with a goal in the centre of each end.

### Court Markings

- **Centre line** — exactly in the middle of the two goal lines, with a centre spot for starting and restarting play
- **Free-throw line (9 m)** — dashed D-shaped line, 9 m from the outer goal line; used to resume play if an attacker is fouled within the area
- **Penalty mark (7 m)** — located 7 m in front of the goal line, 1 m wide; used for all penalty throws
- **Goal Area / Crease / D-Zone (6 m)** — D-shaped area formed by two connected quarter-circles with 6 m radius; only the goalkeeper is allowed in this area
- **Goalkeeper line (4 m)** — goalkeeper cannot pass this point when defending a penalty shot

## 6. Players

A team consists of **7 players per side** (including goalkeeper). All outfield players are interchangeable during the game.

| Position | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Goalkeeper** | Defends the goal |
| **Left Wing** | Covers left side; provides counter-attacks down the left |
| **Right Wing** | Same as left wing on the opposite side |
| **Left Back** | Stands left of centre back; prevents shooting, initiates counter-attacks |
| **Right Back** | Same as left back on the opposite side |
| **Centre Back** | Middle of court; provides defending and attacking options |
| **Pivot** | Travels along opponent's 6 m line; works with centre back to initiate attacks |

## 7. Guest Rule

Maximum **2 guest players** are allowed on the field as per Spectrum regulation. A team can register any number of guest players but only 2 may be on the field at a time.

## 8. Walkover

If a team gives a walkover for a match, **5 goal points** are awarded to the corresponding opponent team.
