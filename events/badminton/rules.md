# 🏸 Badminton Rules — Spectrum '26

## 1. Tournament Format

Each team match consists of five games:

1. Men's Singles
2. Women's Singles
3. Men's Doubles
4. Women's Doubles
5. Mixed Doubles

A team wins the match by winning at least **3 out of 5 games**.

Each game follows a **best-of-3 sets** format in the league stage, with each set played to **21 points**. The scoring format for knockout stages may differ and will be communicated after registrations close.

### Team Composition

- Minimum 4 male players
- Minimum 3 female players
- Maximum 12 players (including guest players)

Teams must submit player details in advance. All players will undergo verification prior to participation. Additional players may be added later provided they have not represented or registered for any other team.

### Guest Player Rules

- Maximum 1 male guest player in the playing team
- Maximum 1 female guest player in the playing team
- Once a guest player has played for a team, they cannot represent any other team
- Only one female player is allowed to repeat in a match
- A female guest player cannot repeat and may play only one game per match

### Pool Stage

- Initial rounds will determine the top 16 teams
- These teams will be divided into 4 pools of 4 teams each (round-robin format)
- Pool structure and qualification criteria may be modified at the discretion of the organizers

## 2. General Rules

- **Official shuttle:** Yonex Mavis 350
- All matches conducted in accordance with **BWF rules**; umpire's decision is final
- Inappropriate behavior may result in point deduction or disciplinary action
- **Non-marking badminton shoes** are mandatory — players without appropriate footwear will not be allowed to play
- Failure to comply with any rules may result in disqualification
- Final decision in all matters rests with the Convenor and GSAC members

### Match Scheduling

- Match schedule shared at least one day in advance
- Changes only permitted with mutual agreement between teams and organizer approval
- Teams must report at scheduled time — failure results in disqualification
- If court closing time approaches with pending matches, they will be rescheduled at organizers' discretion

### Team Conduct

- Each team must appoint one team leader as sole point of contact
- Only the team captain may approach organizers in case of dispute
- Misconduct by other players may lead to disqualification or point deduction
- Teams found deliberately underperforming or manipulating outcomes will be disqualified

### Tiebreak (Pool Standings)

1. Number of matches won
2. Game difference
3. Point difference
4. Head-to-head result
