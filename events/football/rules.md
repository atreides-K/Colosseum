# ⚽ Football Rules — Spectrum '26

**Men's Football** — 13 Teams | **Women's Football** — 5 Teams

## 1. Tournament Format

### Men's

#### Round 1 — Knockouts
- 12 teams play 6 knockout matches. Physics Dept receive a direct bye as defending champions.
- The best-performing losing team among the 6 KO losers advances as the 8th team for the group stage.
- Best loser criteria: Goal Difference → Goals Scored → Cards Received → Penalty Shootout (if still tied).
- Each half: **20 minutes**. No extra time. If tied, straight to penalty shootout.

#### Round 2 — Group Stage (League)
- 8 teams split into 2 groups of 4 (Group A & Group B).
- Each team plays the other 3 teams in their group once.
- Top 2 from each group qualify for the semi-finals.
- Each half: **20 minutes**. No extra time. No penalties. Draws stand.
- Points: **Win = 3 | Draw = 1 | Loss = 0**

**Group Stage Rankings (tiebreak):**

1. Goal Difference
2. Goals Scored
3. Head-to-Head result
4. Fair Play (fewer cards = higher rank)
5. Penalty Shootout

#### Semi-finals
- Semi 1: 1st Group A vs 2nd Group B
- Semi 2: 1st Group B vs 2nd Group A
- Each half: **25 minutes**. If tied: 7+7 minutes extra time, then penalty shootout.

#### Final
- Each half: **25 minutes**. If tied: 7+7 minutes extra time, then penalty shootout.

### Women's
- Round robin (all play all) → Top 2 play Final
- Match duration: **15-minute halves** (30 min total)
- Points: **Win = 3 | Draw = 1 | Loss = 0**

## 2. Playing Rules

- **10 + 1** (goalkeeper) players per side on the field
- A team must field a minimum of **8 players** or forfeit the match
- Standard FIFA offside rules apply
- Standard throw-in rules apply: both feet on the ground and outside the pitch, ball held with both hands and delivered over the head while facing the field
- Minimum distance from ball at free kick: **10 normal steps**. Failure to retreat is punishable by a card
- Each half starts with a kick-off from the centre spot. Captains toss a coin for ends and kick-off

## 3. Goalkeeping

- Goal kick when ball goes out across goal line off an opponent
- After gaining control with hands, the goalkeeper may kick or throw it. Catching, picking up, or patting down = control. Punching away for a save ≠ control
- Goalkeeper **cannot** pick up the ball from a deliberate back-pass or kick-in by a teammate (indirect free kick)
- Goal kicks do not result in a goal unless the ball touches another player (except if the keeper collected and kicked it)
- Opponents cannot enter the D during a goal kick
- Keeper must release the ball within **6 seconds** of controlling it (violation = corner kick)

## 4. Substitutions

- **Rolling substitutions** allowed throughout the tournament
- Only during a break in play, with referee's and match official's consent
- Must take place at the halfway line; player leaving must exit before replacement enters
- Goalkeeper substitution follows the same rolling substitution rule during open play
- Goalkeeper substitution is **NOT** allowed during penalties, penalty shootouts, and free kicks (unless injured)

## 5. Linesman Duty

- Each men's team must provide **2 persons** for linesman duty (captain's responsibility)
- Group A matches covered by a Group B team, and vice versa
- Must report at least **10 minutes** before kick-off
- Failure to provide linesmen: **-1 goal difference** deduction per match missed
- Late arrival after kick-off: warning for first offence, then -1 GD penalty

## 6. Guest Rules

- A team can play at most **4 guests** on the pitch at a time
- Alumni are counted as guests

## 7. Penalty Kicks

Penalties are awarded for:
1. Handball inside the D
2. Foul/obstruction inside the D
3. Any other offence the referee deems punishable inside the D

- Keeper must remain on the line until the ball is struck (at least one foot on the line)
- Goalkeeper can be changed for penalties during open play (not during shootouts unless injured)

**Penalty Shootout:**
1. 5 penalty kicks each, followed by sudden death if still tied
2. Only the 11 players on the field at the final whistle may take part
3. Any of those 11 may serve as goalkeeper for the shootout

## 8. Cards & Discipline

- **Yellow Card:** Caution. Two yellows in a single match = red card
- **Red Card:** Player must leave immediately and is suspended for the next match
- Yellow cards reset after each round (knockouts → group stage → semis/final), unless they accumulate into a red within the same round
- During the group stage, two yellow cards across matches = red card + one-match suspension
- **Last-man tackle/foul** is a direct red card
- Unnecessary aggression, forceful challenges, or tackling from behind → free kick + possible yellow/red

## 9. Walkover

- Walkover if fewer than **8 players** present 15 minutes after scheduled kick-off
- Result: **3-0 win** to present team (3 points, +3 GD). Absent team: 0 points, -3 GD
- If a team quits mid-match: 0-3 loss applied if current GD < 3; otherwise score at time of quitting stands

## 10. General Rules

- Teams must report **15 minutes** before kick-off
- Proper football shoes (non-metallic spikes) compulsory
- **Shin guards compulsory** — failure = yellow card
- Players must cut nails before matches
- No jewellery, watches, Fitbits, or accessories during play
- Goalkeeper gloves arranged by team captain
- Captains mutually decide jersey colours; bibs provided if no agreement
- Foul language or fighting will not be tolerated
- Intimidating or improperly addressing the referee → yellow card or stricter
- Time-wasting → yellow card at referee's discretion, without prior warning
- Referee's decision on all matters is final and binding
- All other standard FIFA rules apply unless otherwise stated
